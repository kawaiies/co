Best Insoles for Nurses and Healthcare Workers: Stay Comfortable on Your Feet All Day

June 17, 2026 by Currex Blog
nurses healthcare workers insoles standing foot fatigue CURREX

If you are a nurse or healthcare worker, you know the struggle: 12-hour shifts, concrete floors, and rarely a moment to sit down. Your feet take the brunt of it.

The right insoles can make a dramatic difference. After testing multiple options and analyzing feedback from healthcare professionals, here are our top recommendations.

Why Insoles Matter for Healthcare Workers

Nurses walk an average of 4-5 miles per shift. That is like running a half marathon every three days on hard hospital floors.

The right insole helps with:

  • Reducing foot fatigue and leg soreness
  • Providing arch support for long hours of standing
  • Cushioning hard hospital floors
  • Preventing plantar fasciitis and shin splints
  • Reducing lower back pain

Tips for Healthcare Workers

  1. Know your arch type - Do the wet test to find your arch profile
  2. Consider your shoes - ActivePro fits most nursing clogs and sneakers
  3. Give it time - Your feet need 3-5 days to adjust to new arch support
  4. Replace regularly - Replace every 6-9 months for optimal support
  5. Match your pain points - Different insoles address different issues

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use running insoles for work? Yes! The CURREX ActivePro is designed for multi-activity use and works great for standing and walking all day.

How long do CURREX insoles last? With daily use, expect 6-9 months before needing replacement.
